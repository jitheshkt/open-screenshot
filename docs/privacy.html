Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2, 2026
🔒

The short version: Open Screenshot does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. Your screenshots and settings never leave your device. There are no analytics, no accounts, and no network requests.

Who this covers

This policy applies to the Open Screenshot browser extension ("the extension"), an open-source tool for capturing and editing screenshots.

What we collect

Nothing. The extension does not collect or receive any personal or usage data. It has no analytics, no telemetry, no tracking, no advertising, and makes no requests to any server. It has no backend at all.

What the extension stores, and where

Everything is stored locally in your own browser, using Chrome's storage:

  • The screenshot you're editing is held temporarily in session storage so it can be handed to the editor, then cleared.
  • Your style preferences (background, colors, padding, shadow, annotation defaults) are saved in local storage so the editor opens the way you left it.

This data stays on your machine, is never uploaded, and can be removed at any time by clearing the extension's storage or uninstalling it.

Permissions, and why they're needed

The extension requests the smallest set of permissions its features require, with no broad host access:

PermissionWhy it's used
activeTab Grants temporary access to the current tab only when you invoke the extension (a toolbar click or keyboard shortcut) — used to capture the page and, for full-page and selection captures, to scroll and draw the selection overlay.
scripting Runs the measure/scroll and selection-overlay logic in that active tab.
storage Temporarily holds the captured image and remembers your style preferences, locally.

Because the extension declares no host_permissions and installs no always-on content script, Chrome does not show the "read and change all your data on all websites" warning.

Third parties

None. The extension does not use any third-party services, SDKs, or analytics providers, and does not share data with anyone — because it has no data to share.

Children's privacy

The extension does not collect data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new "last updated" date. Material changes will be noted in the project's release notes.

Contact

Questions about privacy? Open an issue or a discussion on the project's GitHub repository.
